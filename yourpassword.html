    Why length matters more than complexity

    A 20-character lowercase password has more possible combinations than a 10-character password with uppercase, numbers, and symbols. Every extra character multiplies the search space exponentially — adding a character is always stronger than just adding a new symbol type.

    What is entropy?

    Entropy measures unpredictability in bits. Each bit doubles the number of guesses needed. A password with 60 bits of entropy requires 2⁶⁰ ≈ 1 quadrillion guesses. Modern GPUs can try ~10 billion passwords per second, so aim for 80+ bits of entropy for important accounts.

    Why P@ssw0rd is weak Weak pattern

    Attackers use dictionary attacks with common substitutions (a→@, o→0, e→3) already built in. A password that looks complex but follows a predictable pattern provides far less security than it appears. Avoid: dictionary words with substitutions, keyboard walks (qwerty), and dates.

    How dictionary attacks work

    Attackers don't guess randomly — they use lists of millions of known passwords from previous breaches, then apply mutation rules. If your password appears in the top 10 million leaked passwords, it can be cracked in seconds regardless of how complex it looks.

    The case for passphrases Recommended

    Four random words like correct-horse-battery-staple carry ~44 bits of entropy and are far easier to remember than Xk!9mQ2#. Six words gives ~66 bits — strong enough for most purposes, ideal for master passwords.

    Always use two-factor authentication

    Even a strong password can be phished or leaked in a breach. Two-factor authentication (2FA) means an attacker needs both your password and physical access to your device. Use an authenticator app — not SMS when possible — for your most important accounts.
